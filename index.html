Moments captured by police body camera on the night of May 31st last year, traced

Demonstrations after the death of George Floyed went violent in downtown Boston that night.

I tracked intensive moments between protesters and police that night, captured by body camera footage revealed by The Appeal.

April 2021 | By Yuriko Schumacher

On the night of May 31, 2020, a week after the death of George Floyd, crowds of people took to the streets in downtown Boston to protest against police brutality.

Peaceful demonstrations turned violent as the night went on, while police officers went out in force to control the situation.

News outlets and witnesses widely reported there was an extensive use of tear gas, pepper spray and physical violence by the police.

The Appeal, an online news and commentary website about the legal and social justice system, exclusively obtained a copy of Boston Police officers’ body camera footage on the night of May 31.

In December 2020, The Appeal reported that those footage showed “police officers bragging about attacking protesters and multiple instances of excessive force and the liberal use of pepper spray.”
